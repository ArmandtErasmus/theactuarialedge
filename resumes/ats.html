ATS Keyword Cheat Sheet

Optimise your CV for actuarial and quantitative roles using these role-specific keywords. Focus on including relevant technical, actuarial, and financial terms naturally to maximise ATS compatibility.

Actuarial Analyst

  • Probability, Statistics, Regression, Time-Series
  • Variance, Covariance, Correlation, PCA
  • Machine Learning, Scikit-Learn, TensorFlow, Numpy, Pandas
  • GLM, Predictive Modelling, Financial Analysis
  • Claims, Insurance, Life-Insurance, Long-Term Insurance, Short-Term Insurance
  • Data Cleaning, Outlier Detection, Financial Reporting
  • Risk Factors, Sensitivity Analysis, Pricing, Modelling
  • VaR, ES, Stress Testing, Scenario Analysis
  • SQL, Python, R, Excel, VBA, Regulatory Frameworks, Valuation
  • Prophet, Actuarial Systems, Policy Management Systems, IFRS, GAAP

Quantitative Modelling / Research

  • Stochastic Calculus, Ito Lemma, SDEs
  • Markov Processes, Martingales, Expectations
  • Heston, SABR, Bates, Local Vol Models
  • Monte Carlo Simulation, Variance Reduction
  • Statistical Learning, Feature Engineering
  • Time-Series Modelling: ARIMA, Kalman Filter, State Space
  • Hypothesis Testing, Cross-Validation, Sharpe Ratio
  • Factor Models, Alpha Signals, Backtesting
  • Python, C++, Optimisation, Numerical Methods

Risk / XVA Quant

  • Counterparty Risk, Credit Exposure (EPE, EE)
  • CVA, DVA, FVA, KVA, MVA
  • Sensitivities, Stress Scenarios, Risk Factor Shocks
  • Interest Rate Curves, Multi-Curve Framework
  • Monte Carlo, Path Simulation, Forward Simulation
  • Liquidity Risk, Jump Risk, Wrong-Way Risk
  • Regulatory Frameworks: FRTB, Basel III/IV
  • Model Validation, Benchmarking, Independent Review
  • Python, SQL, Risk Engines, Scenario Generators, C++

How to Use These Keywords

  • Include 12–18 keywords per resume — focus on relevance over quantity
  • Integrate keywords naturally in Skills and Experience sections
  • Tailor keywords to match the specific job description
  • Avoid keyword stuffing; repetition can reduce ATS effectiveness
  • Mix technical, actuarial, and financial terms for balance
  • Highlight achievements with measurable impact alongside keywords

Effective use of relevant keywords can significantly improve your chances of passing ATS screening. Present your skills, experience, and achievements clearly while integrating the most pertinent terms naturally.
